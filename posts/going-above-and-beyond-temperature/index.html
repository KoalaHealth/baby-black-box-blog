Going Above & Beyond: Temperature

2020-12-17
2 min read
Preparation
Going Above & Beyond

My newborn loves 70°F (about 21°C) and starts to fuss at 68°F (20°C) and 72°F (about 22°C).

Today, I slept about two hours. At my wonderful hospital, they requested we watch some educational videos created for new parents, and I stupidly watched ALL of them, including shaken baby syndrome (SBS) and sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS). Since then I’ve had an irrational fear that my newborn may suddenly stop breathing, so I ended up sitting next to the crib all night. Fortunately, this has given me ample time to think, and one thought actually stuck with me: my newborn seemed to stir with changes in temperature.

Using my very “EXTRA” Dyson hot/cold fan, I adjusted temperatures, watched a night cam set on the crib, and recorded when the fussing started. Within a minute of the crib thermometer reporting a temperature of 68°F or 72°F, the groaning and stirring began. This was reproduced a few times.

When I shared this observation with my wife, she suprisingly noticed similar results. She said when she set the Dyson at around 72°F so the temperature reported 70°F at the crib, the baby slept, well, like a baby.

This of course is far from scientific. Still, it was an observation I thought worth sharing. I think one alternative interpretation of this micro study is simply timing. The time it took for the room to cool or heat may have coincided well with the baby’s sleeping+eating schedule.

Still, I think it may be worth starting at 70°F, adjust, and see if it helps your bundle of joy sleep!
